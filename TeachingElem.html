Unit of Inquiry for 1st through 3rd grade

I had the great opportunity to collaboratively create a completely original and in-depth learning experience for a wonderful group of students:


Learning about windpower and interactive design.
  • Investigate how wind is generated
  • Investigate wind-powered vehicles
  • Engineer wind-powered vehicles through an interactive design process (completely student-driven designs)
Investgating how buildings are designed and constructed to serve different communities.
  • Review the basic features of our houses
  • Investigate climate and the natural resources available to different communities
  • Investigate how buildings serve the different cultural demands
  • Investigate the properties of different construction materials (tensile and compressive strength)
  • Design villages and buildings to meet the needs of different cultures and locations
  • Build an actually building with students
Investigating the nature of number systems
  • Learn to use ancient Roman, Chinese, Egyptian, and Babylonian number systems
  • Investigate the different ways cultures have measured time
  • Learn how to count in any base (base 2, 5, 6, 8, 10, 60, or any other)
  • design their own symbols for numbers using any base they want
  • Make a real clock which uses their symbols and number system
Investigate land use, natural resources, and pollution
  • Understand the percentages of habitable land use
  • Investigate the dangers of pollution like biomagifcation
  • Plan for better use
  • Investigate water management and processing
Investigate evolution with xenobiology
  • Investigate the process of evolution through natural selection
  • Investigate convergent evolution in different ecological niches
  • Investigate the evolution of animals in different trophic levels
  • Imagine ecosystems on an alien planet
